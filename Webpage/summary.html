Summary

Conclusions

After analyzing our data through visual, statistical, and predictive analysis we found that there is not a strong correlation between US migration and the price of housing. This disproves the theory that population change is not a leading factor in increases in the housing market. Although we did not see much causation in our analysis, we are still curious to learn more about what is causing housing market increases.

Limitations

A large limitation we noticed with the data was that our migration data only had information from 2010 to 2019, which meant that data from 2020 to 2022 was not compared to real estate marked data. It is possible that, as a result of COVID, our result could be different, but it's not likely based on the trends we observed.

Scott Armstrong, Brian Kenney, Alberto Garcia, Jadon Gonzaga
